What Is the NFL?

The National Football League (NFL) is a professional American football league made up of 32 teams divided between the American Football Conference (AFC) and National Football Conference (NFC).

Each season culminates in the Super Bowl, where the AFC and NFC champions meet to compete for the league title.

Quick League Facts

  • 32 Teams (16 in the AFC, 16 in the NFC)
  • Regular season typically spans 18 weeks
  • Playoffs lead to the Super Bowl

How the Game Flows

  1. Kickoff starts the game.
  2. Offense has four downs to gain 10 yards (resetting to a new first down if successful).
  3. Teams score via touchdowns, field goals, and extra points/two-point conversions.
  4. Defense tries to stop drives, force punts, or create turnovers.

Downs and distance are the core mechanics: move the ball 10+ yards in four tries to keep the drive alive.
